LINE TRACER

Is your bot, a master in tracing lines?

PROBLEM STATEMENT 

To build an autonomous robot capable of following a black line on a white surface or white line on black surface in the shortest period of time.

General Rules: 
 

1. A team can consist of maximum 4 members.

2. The candidates may or may not be from different colleges.

3. The members must bring IDENTITY CARDS of the college.

4. No person should be a member of two teams.

5. No two teams should participate with the same robot for this event.

6. Any kind of damage to the arena by the robot will lead to direct

disqualification.

7. Any act of misbehaviour or misconduct will lead to immediate

disqualification of the team.

8. The decision of the coordinators will be final and binding.

9. Any or all of the rules are subject to change at any point of time.

Event Rules:

1. The event consists of two rounds â€“ Prelims and Finals.

2. The arena will have a predefined starting zone or line and a stopping zone or line.

3. The timer will stop as soon as the bot reaches the end of arena where if the bot stops on its own, bonus points will be awarded.

4. There may be some short line discontinuities in either of the two rounds.

5. Whenever the bot reaches a checkpoint (line intersection of length 8 cm approx.) it is supposed to indicate that with a suitable indicator (LED, Buzzer, etc.) which should be clearly noticeable to the event head. This indicator should be off all other times.

6. The robot has to be developed and designed by the team itself. The usage of commercially available modules (sensors, development boards, etc.) is allowed, but it is forbidden to use a complete robotic system which is sold ready for the purpose of the competition (like LEGO kits).

7. Hard Coding is strictly prohibited.

8. Bots of the participant may be checked anytime for above mentioned criteria.

9. The maximum dimensions of the bot should be 22cm x 22cm x 22cm.
