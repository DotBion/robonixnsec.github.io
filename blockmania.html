BLOCKMANIA

Can your line follower keep a track of blocks too? 

PROBLEM STATEMENT 

PRELIMS:- 
To build a line following robot capable of counting white blocks of same dimensions present parallel to the side of the line followed by the bot.  
FINALS:-
To build a line following robot capable of distinguishing and counting white blocks of two different lengths present parallel to the side of the line followed by the bot.

General Rules: 
 
1. A team can consist of maximum 4 members.
2. The candidates may or may not be from different colleges.
3. The members must bring IDENTITY CARDS of the college.
4. No person should be a member of two teams. 
5. No two teams should participate with the same robot for this event.
6. Any kind of damage to the arena by the robot will lead to direct disqualification.
7. Any act of misbehaviour or misconduct will lead to immediate disqualification of the team. 
8. Damaging the path will lead to immediate disqualification.
9. The decision of the coordinators will be final and binding.
10. Any or all of the rules are subject to change at any point of time.

Event Rules:

1. In prelims, the robot has to start following the line from the start point, and indicate (by a LED or Buzzer) when it passes by a white block (L x B x H = 5cm x 5cm x 5cm) on one side of the line, keep a count of the number of blocks and show it by blinking a LED or a Buzzer (that number of times with a 1 sec delay between successive blinks) when the robot reaches the end of arena (rectangular patch) where it must stop before showing the count.

2. In finals, there will be two types of white blocks (differing in length only) â€“ small blocks with dimensions (L x B x H = 5cm x 5cm x 5cm) and large blocks with dimensions (L x B x H = 10cm x 5cm x 5cm). The robot has to start following the line, indicate (by Two Different LEDs or One LED and a Buzzer) which type of block it just passed by. It also has to keep two separate counts for the no. of small and large blocks which has to be shown by blinking the two LEDs or one LED and a Buzzer that no. of times with a 1 sec delay between successive blinks. First the number of small blocks then the no. of large blocks. Here also this thing has to be done after the robot reaches the end of arena (rectangular patch) where it must stop.

3. The timer will stop just at the moment when the bot stops at the end of arena. The time spent in showing the no. of blocks by blinking LED will not be added to the bot run time.

4. The participant has to clearly specify which LED or Buzzer is for indicating which type of block prior to the final run, to the Event Head.

5. The blocks will only be present parallel to straight line portions of the arena.

6. The prelims arena may contain checkpoints or intersections (of length max. 8 cm) which needs to be indicated by blinking that same LED or Buzzer.

7. The maximum time allotted to complete the task is 4 minutes in the prelims and 6 minutes in the finals.

8. The width of line would be 2.5cm approx.

9. The maximum dimensions of the bot should be 22cm x 22cm x 22cm.
