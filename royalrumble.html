ROYAL RUMBLE

Do you have the strength to push others out?

PROBLEM STATEMENT 

To build a manual robot capable of pushing or immobilizing other robots
without destroying it.

General Rules: 
 

1. A team can consist of maximum 4 members.
2. The candidates may or may not be from different colleges.
3. The members must bring IDENTITY CARDS of the college.
4. No person should be a member of two teams.
5. No two teams should participate with the same robot for this event.
6. Any kind of damage to the arena by the robot will lead to direct
disqualification.
7. Any act of misbehaviour or misconduct will lead to immediate
disqualification of the team.
8. Damaging the path will lead to immediate disqualification.
9. The decision of the coordinators will be final and binding.
10. Any or all of the rules are subject to change at any point of time.
11.It’s an all-day event.
12.A team can participate any number of times by paying a small entry fee
for each round.

Event Rules:


1. The main objective of the event is to immobilize the opponent’s robot
without destroying or breaking it.
2. The participant has three ways to immobilize it :-
(a) By pushing the opponent out of the arena.
(b) By pushing the opponent into the pit.
(c) By flipping it in such a way that it can’t move.
3. Destruction of the opponent’s robot in anyway will lead to direct
disqualification and the team will not be allowed to take part in any of
the subsequent rounds.
4. Mechanisms used in war bots can be applied, provided that it doesn’t
destroy the opponent’s bot.
5. Holding or blocking the opponent in any corner of the arena or any place
inside the arena will not be considered as immobilization.
6. If, while pushing the opponent out of the arena, the participant itself
moves out of the arena then he/she is also considered to be
immobilised.
7. The round is deemed to be complete if all but one team is immobilised.
8. A robot is immobilised once more than 60% of the wheels is outside the
arena or inside the pit.
9. The winner of a particular round gets instant cash money.
10.The winner can’t take part in succession. The team has to wait for one
round to complete before he is eligible to take part again in the next
round.
11.Each round will consist of 6 or 8 robots whichever is feasible at that
point of time.
12.All participants are requested to be alert at all times for the call of pause
and resume in-case required to untangle the wires.
13.Bot dimension (25 x 25 x 25) cm with 10% tolerance.
14.Maximum voltage between any two points is 24 V (which may be
checked if required).
15.Maximum weight of the bot is STRICTLY 2 KG.
16.AC 220 V will be provided, any other required power sources, extension
cords and power adaptors should be carried by the participant.
17.No use of any Lego or readymade bots; use of so will lead to direct
disqualification.
18.Bots of the participant may be checked anytime; before, during or after
the run for above mentioned criteria.
19.No use of any unfair means, if found will lead to immediate
disqualification.
20.All participants are requested to maintain the spirit of a healthy
competition.
